# My Favorite Music & Why I Love It

---

## The Weeknd
*Favorite songs: Save Your Tears · Popular*

What I love about The Weeknd is the consistency. When I put on one of his songs I know exactly what I'm walking into — that smooth, slightly melancholic atmosphere, the tempo that gets your head moving without demanding your full attention. Save Your Tears is one of those songs that just works every single time, no matter the mood. Popular hits differently, that effortless cool he carries into every track. He's one of those artists where the whole discography feels like one coherent world he built and invites you into.

---

## Bruno Mars
*Favorite songs: Risk It All · Locked Out of Heaven*

Bruno Mars is one of the most reliable artists in music. Every song he puts out has that same infectious energy — the kind where the tempo grabs you before the lyrics even register. Risk It All has this warmth to it that feels timeless, and Locked Out of Heaven is just pure joy from start to finish. He sits in that pop-soul space where everything grooves and nothing ever feels forced. I always know what I'm getting with Bruno Mars and that's exactly why I keep coming back.

---

## America
*Favorite songs: A Horse With No Name · Ventura Highway · Magic*

I've been listening to a lot of America lately and it scratches a completely different itch. There's something about the simplicity of the guitar and the way the lyrics actually tell a story that feels rare now. A Horse With No Name has this open, drifting quality to it — you feel the desert and the silence just from the chord progression. Ventura Highway is effortlessly beautiful. Magic has that same understated warmth. These are songs that don't rush you. They just sit with you and let the story breathe.

---

## Teddy Swims
*Favorite songs: The Door · Bad Dreams · Mr. Know It All · Need You More*

Teddy Swims fits right into the same headspace as America for me. The Door and Bad Dreams both lean hard into the guitar and the lyrics carry genuine weight — the kind of songwriting where you feel like he actually lived what he's singing about. Mr. Know It All and Need You More pull back from the guitar a bit but they hit just as hard in a different way. He's been a recent discovery that I keep returning to. There's a rawness to his voice that makes everything feel more honest.

---

## Coldplay
*Favorite songs: Viva la Vida · Adventure of a Lifetime · Hymn for the Weekend · We Pray*

Coldplay is one of those bands that has genuinely been part of my life since I was old enough to listen to music. Viva la Vida is my favorite — there's something about that orchestral build and the way the lyrics feel both grand and personal that never gets old. Adventure of a Lifetime has an energy that feels like pure momentum. Hymn for the Weekend is beautiful. We Pray hits differently every time. They've managed to stay relevant across decades without losing whatever makes them distinctly Coldplay, and that's harder than it sounds.

---

## Charlie Puth
*Favorite songs: LA Girls · Attention · How Long · Done for Me · December 25th*

Charlie Puth is the outlier in my listening habits and I love him for it. Most of the artists I gravitate toward are consistent — you know the vibe before the song starts. Charlie Puth constantly surprises me. The Voicenotes album in particular feels like it shifts styles across tracks in a way that should feel inconsistent but somehow works because his voice and his production instincts tie it all together. Attention, How Long, Done for Me — each one feels like a different side of the same artist.

And yes, I listen to December 25th outside of the holiday season. I know. I'm not sorry.

---

## Michael Jackson
*Favorite songs: Off the Wall · Chicago · Billie Jean*

I hadn't listened to much Jackson in a while and recently came back to him — and it's been a revelation all over again. Billie Jean is just untouchable. The bassline, the build, the vocal performance — everything about it is perfect. Off the Wall has this irresistible looseness to it, pure joy in musical form. Chicago has been stuck in my head for weeks. What strikes me coming back to his catalog is how timeless it all sounds. Nothing about it feels dated. He was operating on a completely different level.

---

## Soundtracks & Scores
*The Last of Us Part II · God of War · Project Hail Mary · Interstellar*

This is a huge part of how I listen to music and I don't think it gets talked about enough. The Last of Us Part II score by Gustavo Santaolalla is something I return to constantly — that minimal guitar work carries so much weight without a single lyric. The God of War soundtracks by Bear McCreary shift between these enormous orchestral moments and quiet, intimate themes in a way that mirrors the story perfectly.

The Project Hail Mary score and Hans Zimmer's Interstellar soundtrack occupy a special place for me because they put you in a creative headspace unlike anything else. No lyrics, no structure to follow — just music that makes your mind start building the movie again from scratch. I'll put on the Interstellar organ sequences when I need to focus or think deeply about something. There's something about instrumental scores tied to stories you love that hits differently than any other kind of music.
